Motivation: AI & Storage

Why GPUs are at the heart of AI and why storage must evolve to keep pace with the unprecedented demands of modern AI workloads.

The AI Training Pipeline

Modern AI training is a data-hungry process. Models must continuously stream training data from storage to GPU memory. When storage can't keep up, expensive GPUs sit idle.

Checkpoint Challenge

A 1.8T parameter model requires ~7 TB per checkpoint. Training runs save checkpoints every few hours for fault tolerance. With thousands of GPUs, checkpoint I/O can take 10+ minutes ‚Äî during which all GPUs are blocked.

Why NVMe Must Evolve

NVMe was designed in the CPU era, optimized for CPU-attached storage with interrupt-driven completion. GPUs have fundamentally different requirements:

Direct GPU Access
Data should flow NVMe ‚Üí GPU without CPU copies
Polling, Not Interrupts
GPU SIMT model can't handle interrupt-driven I/O
Massive Parallelism
Thousands of concurrent I/O operations per GPU
Large I/O Sizes
Optimal at 1MB+ to amortize per-I/O overhead
